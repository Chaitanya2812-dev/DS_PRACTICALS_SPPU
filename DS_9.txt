# 1
import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import pandas as pd

# 2
df=sns.load_dataset("titanic")
print(df.head())
print(df.info())

# 3
sns.boxplot(x="age",y="sex",hue="survived",data=df)
plt.title("age wrt to gender and survied")
plt.xlabel("gender")
plt.ylabel("age")
plt.show()


📊 Key Observations (Write in Exam)
✅ 1. Females had higher survival
In the female group, many orange boxes (survived) are clearly visible
Indicates more females survived than males
✅ 2. Males had lower survival rate
In the male group, blue (not survived) dominates
Shows many males did not survive
✅ 3. Age distribution is similar
Median age (middle line in box) for both survived and not survived is around 25–35 years
So, age was not a strong factor alone
✅ 4. Presence of outliers
Some points above ~70–80 age
These are outliers (very old passengers)
✅ 5. Spread of data
Male age distribution is slightly wider (longer whiskers)
Indicates more variation in male age
